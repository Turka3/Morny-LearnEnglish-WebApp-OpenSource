Vocabulary

Absurd - wildly unreasonable or illogical
Benevolent - well meaning and kindly
Clandestine - kept secret or done secretly
Diligent - having or showing care and conscientiousness in one's work or duties
Elusive - difficult to find, catch, or achieve
Fervent - having or showing intense feeling or enthusiasm
Gracious - courteous, kind, and pleasant
Haughty - arrogantly superior and disdainful
Impetuous - acting or done quickly and without thought or care
Jovial - cheerful and friendly
Kinetic - relating to or resulting from motion
Luminous - full of or shedding light; bright or shining, especially in the dark
Magnitude - the great size or extent of something
Nascent - just coming into existence and beginning to display signs of future potential
Obscure - not discovered or known about; uncertain
Pensive - engaged in deep or serious thought
Quell - put an end to (a rebellion or other disorder), typically by the use of force
Resilient - able to withstand or recover quickly from difficult conditions
Sanguine - optimistic or positive, especially in an apparently bad or difficult situation
Tenacious - tending to keep a firm hold of something; clinging or adhering closely
Ubiquitous - present, appearing, or found everywhere
Venerable - accorded a great deal of respect, especially because of age, wisdom, or character
Whimsical - playfully quaint or fanciful, especially in an appealing and amusing way
Xenophobic - having or showing a dislike of or prejudice against people from other countries
Yearning - a feeling of intense longing for something
Zenith - the time at which something is most powerful or successful
Ambivalent - having mixed feelings or contradictory ideas about something or someone
Bellicose - demonstrating aggression and willingness to fight
Complacent - showing smug or uncritical satisfaction with oneself or one's achievements
Derisive - expressing contempt or ridicule
Eloquent - fluent or persuasive in speaking or writing
Flippant - not showing a serious or respectful attitude
Gregarious - fond of company; sociable
Harbinger - a person or thing that announces or signals the approach of another
Impervious - not allowing fluid to pass through; unable to be affected by
Jaded - tired, bored, or lacking enthusiasm, typically after having too much of something
Knack - an acquired or natural skill at performing a task
Loquacious - tending to talk a great deal; talkative
Melancholy - a feeling of pensive sadness, typically with no obvious cause
Nuance - a subtle difference in or shade of meaning, expression, or sound
Onerous - involving a great deal of effort, difficulty, or expense
Pithy - concise and forcefully expressive
Quandary - a state of perplexity or uncertainty over what to do in a difficult situation
Resolute - admirably purposeful, determined, and unwavering
Sagacious - having or showing good judgement; shrewd
Taciturn - (of a person) reserved or uncommunicative in speech; saying little
Unassailable - unable to be attacked, questioned
